About the Journal

A scholarly publication focused on biological products

The journal presents research and expert discussion across the development and use of human biological products, bringing together scientific, clinical, methodological, and regulatory perspectives in one specialist publishing space.

Biological Products. Prevention, Diagnosis, Treatment is an open-access, peer-reviewed journal founded by the Federal State Budgetary Institution “Scientific Centre for Expert Evaluation of Medicinal Products” of the Ministry of Health of the Russian Federation.

Its editorial scope addresses regulation, standardization, quality control, production, and clinical application in areas related to therapeutic, prophylactic, and diagnostic biological products, as well as biomedical cell-based technologies and associated scientific methods.

Over twenty-five years, the journal has served as a specialist publication for readers and authors working across biomedical research, laboratory practice, clinical translation, and expert evaluation.

The anniversary offers an opportunity not only to look back, but also to observe the journal as a continuing forum for scientific exchange in a field where product quality, regulatory clarity, and translational relevance remain closely intertwined.

Today, the journal’s identity rests on thematic depth, editorial continuity, open access, and a commitment to scholarly communication that remains accessible to both specialist and emerging research communities.

Journal Today

Continuity of mission in a changing scientific landscape

The present-day journal continues to publish work across research, review, methodology, and commentary formats, preserving thematic continuity while engaging with contemporary biomedical priorities.

What the journal publishes

  • Reviews, including narrative reviews and systematic reviews with meta-analysis
  • Original research and controlled clinical trials
  • Case reports and scientific methodology articles
  • Editorials, commentaries, and responses to published discussion
  • Manuscripts submitted in Russian or English within a specialist scholarly framework

Why the journal remains relevant

Biological products continue to demand careful attention to research quality, product characterization, laboratory evaluation, regulatory reasoning, and clinical application. The journal remains relevant because it publishes at those intersections rather than within only one disciplinary silo.

Its continued value lies in bringing together scientific evidence, methodological reflection, and publication formats that remain meaningful for researchers, expert institutions, and professional audiences working in adjacent domains.
